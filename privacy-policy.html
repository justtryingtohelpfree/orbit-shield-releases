🛡️ Orbit Browser

Privacy Policy
Last updated: March 18, 2026
TL;DR: Orbit Browser collects zero user data. No analytics, no tracking, no telemetry, no accounts. Everything stays on your device.

1. Our Philosophy

Orbit Browser is built on a simple principle: your browsing data belongs to you, and only you. We designed Orbit to be the anti-surveillance browser. We don't collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal information whatsoever.

2. Data We Collect

NONE

Orbit Browser does not collect any data. Specifically:

  • No analytics or telemetry — we don't track how you use the app
  • No crash reporting — crash data stays on your device
  • No user accounts — there is nothing to sign up for
  • No browsing history sync — your history never leaves your phone
  • No advertising IDs — we don't integrate any ad SDKs
  • No fingerprinting — in fact, we actively block fingerprinting on websites you visit

3. Data Stored Locally on Your Device

Orbit stores the following data locally on your device only. This data never leaves your phone:

  • Browsing history — stored in a local database, clearable at any time
  • Bookmarks — saved locally in app storage
  • Saved passwords — encrypted using Android's hardware-backed Keystore (AES-256-GCM) and stored in an encrypted database
  • Vault tabs — biometric-locked private browsing sessions, encrypted at rest
  • Extension settings — ad-blocker preferences stored locally
  • Cookies & site data — managed by the browser engine (GeckoView), clearable at any time

4. Built-In Privacy Features

Orbit actively protects your privacy while you browse:

  • Ad & Tracker Blocking — blocks ads, trackers, and malicious scripts at both the network and DOM level
  • Fingerprint Protection — randomizes canvas, WebGL, and audio fingerprints to prevent cross-site tracking
  • Cookie Banner Auto-Reject — automatically dismisses GDPR cookie consent banners, choosing the most private option
  • Tracking Parameter Stripping — removes tracking parameters (fbclid, utm_*, gclid, etc.) from URLs
  • Encrypted Password Vault — passwords are encrypted with AES-256-GCM using hardware-backed keys

5. Network Connections

Orbit Browser makes the following network connections:

  • Website requests — when you navigate to a URL (standard browser behavior)
  • Extension update check — on app launch, Orbit checks for ad-blocker updates from our servers (a small JSON manifest). No user data is sent with this request — only the app version.
  • Search queries — sent to your selected search engine (DuckDuckGo by default)

We do not operate any analytics servers, tracking pixels, or data collection endpoints.

6. Third-Party Services

Orbit Browser does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services. Specifically:

  • No Google Analytics
  • No Firebase
  • No Facebook SDK
  • No Crashlytics
  • No advertising SDKs

The browser engine (Mozilla GeckoView) is used for rendering web pages. GeckoView is open-source and does not collect user data in our configuration.

7. Children's Privacy

Orbit Browser does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13. Since we collect no data from anyone, this applies universally.

8. Data Security

All sensitive data stored by Orbit is encrypted:

  • Passwords use AES-256-GCM with Android Keystore-backed keys
  • Vault tabs are encrypted at rest and require biometric authentication
  • All local databases can be wiped with a single action

9. Your Rights

Since we don't collect any data, there is nothing to request, correct, or delete from our servers. You have full control over all local data through the app's settings — you can clear browsing history, cookies, saved passwords, and all app data at any time.

10. Open Source

Orbit Browser's ad-blocking engine is open source. You can audit exactly what code runs on your device. We believe transparency is the foundation of trust.

11. Changes to This Policy

If we ever update this privacy policy, changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. Our commitment to collecting zero data will not change.
